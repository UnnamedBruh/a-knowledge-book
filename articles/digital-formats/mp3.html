Overview

MP3 (formally referred to as MPEG-1 Layer III Encoding) is a digital lossy audio format, which uses psychoacoustic models to discard frequencies not perceptible to the human ear, to ensure the audio is stored concisely. The format comes with quality loss, where file sizes are a priority, and preserving the original quality is not a concern.

Is It Patented?

Historically, the MP3 format was designed by the Moving Pictures Expert Group (commonly known as MPEG), and historically patented by Technicolor, a company which specializes in filmmaking and technologies. The last license (relating to the MP3 structure) expired on April 27th, 2017. So, no, it is no longer patented, and you are free to write an MP3 audio encoder/decoder without worrying about legal issues.
