The examples below provide indirect or direct uses of this term ("time").

Noun:
  • the continued progress of existence, including past, present and future events. "this object's lifetime"
  • a temporary period of nothingness. "I have waited for a few seconds"
  • a philosophical concept, typically accounting delays, temporary idle events, and history of any occurrings. "time is an illusion..." - Albert Einstein
  • a measured unit with certain value; (measurement of) duration of an event. "I have waited for some time"
  • an individual perception of duration. "today feels quick. How about you?"
  • a perception of changing duration within a given timeframe. "I feel a sudden increase in time..."
  • a specified period; when an event occurs, or has occurred. "in 1861, The Civil War began"
  • an indirect, past (memorable or notable) period. "his time is incredibly different from now"
  • an occurrence indirectly conveyed. "this is the first time I've seen him"
  • a visual representation of current progress (within a day), typically from clocks or watches. "on my watch, the time is 6:30"
  • (in music) tempo. "the song's timing is 200 BPM"
  • (in question) a certain progress of the current period. "what time is it?"
Verb:
  • to record or keep track of a duration of a present or future tense event. "time me; let's see if I can break this record!"
  • to schedule an event. "he timed his meeting to happen at 1:00"
Adjective:
  • precisely occurring. "this bottle flip was well-timed"
  • precise in duration. "his speed was quickly-timed"
  • limited in duration. "I was timed; I needed to complete this challenge in mere minutes"
Plural:
  • number of occurrences of a specified event. "I met Harris three times this morning"
Exclamation:
  • (in competitive, or urgent contexts) a phrase conveying an end to an event. "time's up!"
Expression:
  • expressing a rushed, urgent or quick mood. "I don't have time for that!"
  • indirectly expressing a future possibility. "now is not the time!"
  • marking a near-future possibility. "it's time"
  • expressing frustration of a timeframe not meaningfully used. "come on, you are wasting time!"
